How have the beats been bangin' so far.

Hey, so I've been a music lover since childhood and I love to listen and rap to beats as it calms my brain and helps me to express what just couldn't have been possible without it. I don't consider myself a true audiophile but I rather have a taste of my own and it hasn't changed a lot in all these years. (Background - Tomorrowland).

Rap - The genre that I've been vibing the most to.

I have been listening to a lot of international rappers like Eminem, G-Eazy, Tupac, Joyner Lucas, Kendrick Lamar, Drake, Logic, J.Cole, Kanye West, Pop Smoke and Russ, just to name a few. The lyrical complexity and the rhyme scheme always intrigued me and I fell in love with the way all of them tell a story knit the words to make the listener actually imagine all of it, just by the means of listening, a skill not a lot of people can conquer.The modern rap scene is a vibrant and ever-evolving landscape that encompasses a diverse array of artists, styles, and influences. At the forefront of this dynamic movement are several iconic figures who have left an indelible mark on the genre, each with their own unique contributions and distinct voices. From G-Eazy's infectious beats to Eminem's raw storytelling, Joyner Lucas's intricate wordplay to Logic's introspective lyricism, J. Cole's soulful rhymes to Kanye West's innovative production, these artists collectively shape the cultural zeitgeist and inspire generations of fans around the world. G-Eazy, born Gerald Earl Gillum, rose to prominence in the early 2010s with his distinctive blend of hip-hop, pop, and electronic music. His debut album, "These Things Happen," catapulted him into the mainstream spotlight, featuring hit singles like "Me, Myself & I" and "I Mean It." G-Eazy's smooth delivery, catchy hooks, and relatable lyrics have resonated with audiences worldwide, earning him a dedicated fanbase and critical acclaim. With subsequent albums like "When It's Dark Out" and "The Beautiful & Damned," he has continued to evolve his sound while staying true to his roots, solidifying his status as one of the leading voices in contemporary rap. Eminem, also known as Slim Shady or Marshall Mathers, is widely regarded as one of the greatest rappers of all time. His unparalleled lyrical prowess, raw storytelling, and provocative persona have made him a cultural icon and a dominant force in the rap world for over two decades. From his groundbreaking debut album, "The Slim Shady LP," to his critically acclaimed releases like "The Marshall Mathers LP" and "The Eminem Show," Eminem has consistently pushed the boundaries of the genre, tackling controversial subjects and challenging societal norms with his unapologetic honesty and intensity. Joyner Lucas, born Gary Maurice Lucas Jr., has emerged as one of the most exciting talents in contemporary rap with his intricate wordplay, thought-provoking narratives, and dynamic delivery. His breakout single, "I'm Not Racist," sparked widespread acclaim and conversation for its powerful exploration of race relations in America. Since then, Joyner Lucas has continued to push the boundaries of the genre with his emotionally charged lyrics and socially conscious themes, earning him a reputation as a fearless storyteller and a voice for the voiceless. Logic, born Sir Robert Bryson Hall II, has captivated audiences with his introspective lyricism, soulful beats, and socially conscious rhymes. His debut album, "Under Pressure," showcased his lyrical prowess and personal storytelling, while subsequent releases like "The Incredible True Story" and "Everybody" further solidified his status as a rising star in the rap world. Logic's candid exploration of mental health, identity, and societal issues has resonated with fans, inspiring them to embrace their own individuality and confront their inner demons with courage and authenticity. J. Cole, born Jermaine Lamarr Cole, has become synonymous with soulful beats, introspective lyricism, and thought-provoking storytelling. From his debut mixtape, "The Come Up," to his critically acclaimed albums like "2014 Forest Hills Drive" and "KOD," J. Cole has carved out a niche for himself as one of the most respected voices in contemporary rap. His socially conscious rhymes, personal anecdotes, and introspective musings have earned him a dedicated fanbase and critical acclaim, cementing his legacy as one of the genre's most influential artists. Kanye West, also known as Ye or Yeezy, is a visionary producer, artist, and cultural icon who has continuously pushed the boundaries of rap with his innovative production, genre-blending experimentation, and provocative lyricism. From his groundbreaking debut album, "The College Dropout," to his genre-defying releases like "Yeezus" and "My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy," Kanye West has reshaped the landscape of modern music and inspired countless artists to push the boundaries of creativity and self-expression. Despite his controversial public persona and headline-grabbing antics, Kanye West remains one of the most influential and impactful figures in contemporary rap, leaving an indelible mark on the genre and inspiring generations of fans and artists alike. In conclusion, the modern rap scene is a dynamic and multifaceted landscape shaped by the diverse talents and unique contributions of artists like G-Eazy, Eminem, Joyner Lucas, Logic, J. Cole, and Kanye West. Each artist brings their own distinct style and perspective to the genre, pushing the boundaries of creativity and self-expression while inspiring audiences with their authenticity, honesty, and unwavering commitment to their craft. Together, they represent the vanguard of contemporary rap, shaping the cultural zeitgeist and inspiring generations of fans around the world.

The likes of The Weeknd, Drake, Travis Scott, Lana Del Rey, Selena Gomez, Charlie Puth, and Bebe Rexha have made significant contributions to the realms of R&B and pop music. His genre-defying sound resonates with listeners across the globe, earning him critical acclaim and commercial success. Similarly, Drake has emerged as one of the most influential figures in contemporary hip-hop and R&B. Similarly, Travis Scott has pushed the boundaries of rap with his psychedelic production and hypnotic flow, earning him a devoted fanbase and widespread recognition for his innovative sound. On the pop music front, Lana Del Rey, Selena Gomez, Charlie Puth, and Bebe Rexha have captivated audiences with their distinctive styles and undeniable talent. Lana Del Rey's cinematic soundscapes and dreamy vocals have earned her critical acclaim, carving out a niche for herself in the indie-pop scene. Selena Gomez, with her sultry vocals and infectious pop anthems, has become one of the biggest pop stars of her generation, dominating the charts with hits like "Come & Get It" and "Good for You." Charlie Puth, known for his soulful vocals and heartfelt songwriting, has emerged as a formidable force in the pop world, earning widespread acclaim for his chart-topping hits and undeniable talent. Similarly, Bebe Rexha has established herself as one of the most versatile and dynamic artists in the pop scene, collaborating with some of the biggest names in music and earning a devoted fanbase with her catchy hooks and relatable lyrics. In addition to these talented artists, the EDM scene of the mid-2010s witnessed a surge of talent and innovation, with DJs like Skrillex, Diplo, Marshmello, Slushi, Alan Walker, K391, Martin Garrix, and David Guetta leading the charge. Skrillex, known for his groundbreaking dubstep sound and energetic live performances, was instrumental in popularizing electronic dance music in mainstream culture, earning him multiple Grammy Awards and a devoted fanbase. Diplo, with his eclectic blend of electronic, hip-hop, and world music influences, has become one of the most sought-after DJs and producers in the industry, collaborating with artists across genres and pushing the boundaries of dance music with his innovative production. Marshmello, with his signature marshmallow helmet and infectious beats, has become a global phenomenon, dominating the charts with his catchy pop-infused EDM tracks and high-energy live shows. Alan Walker, K391, and Martin Garrix have also made significant contributions to the EDM scene, each bringing their own unique style and sound to the genre and inspiring a new generation of producers and DJs with their innovative approach to electronic music.
